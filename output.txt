Case Summary
On 21 March 2024 (Day 128 of treatment with Cardivex 10mg), the patient, Mr. Suresh Balakrishnan Iyer (58M), presented with a 3-day history of progressively worsening bilateral lower limb weakness and cola-coloured urine. Investigations revealed significantly elevated Serum Creatine Kinase (12,400 U/L), Serum Creatinine (2.1 mg/dL), and positive myoglobinuria, leading to a diagnosis of Statin-induced Rhabdomyolysis with Acute Kidney Injury (AKI). The study drug was immediately discontinued, and aggressive IV hydration was initiated. The patient's condition improved, with CK and creatinine levels trending towards normal. Causality was assessed as PROBABLE. The patient was discharged on Day 8 (28 March 2024) recovering, with residual mild proximal muscle weakness. A 30-day follow-up is planned.

Overall Assessment
The inspection identified a significant number of deficiencies, including five critical findings, indicating a pervasive lack of adherence to Good Manufacturing Practices as stipulated in Schedule M of the Drugs & Cosmetics Act 1940. The critical findings, particularly regarding cross-contamination risk, data integrity, OOS investigation failures, and compromised raw material quality, pose immediate and serious risks to product quality and patient safety. The facility is deemed Non-Compliant with current GMP requirements.
